🔥 Are Wildfire Seasons Expanding?

Creative visualizations testing the "starting earlier, lasting longer" hypothesis

1. Season Length Over Time

What it shows: The calendar span of each fire season (start date to end date) plotted over time. If seasons are "lasting longer," this line should trend upward. The x-axis shows the calendar date when the season starts; the y-axis shows total season length in days.
💡 Hover over points to see exact dates. Watch for the trend: are later years expanding?

2. Season Boundaries: When Do Fires Start & End?

What it shows: A slope chart showing when each fire season officially begins and ends (defined as crossing 500 detections threshold). Lines going left = earlier start; lines going right = later end. Steeper slopes = longer seasons.
💡 Red = recent years (2023-2025), Blue = early years (2018-2020). Notice if red lines slope differently than blue lines.

3. Season Intensity Profile: How Does Fire Activity Spread Through the Season?

What it shows: Normalized fire activity across the 5-month season for each year. Think of it like a "heatwave" shape. If the profile is getting wider/flatter, the season is extending. If the tail is extending into October, that's "lasting longer."
💡 Early years should peak sharply in July-August. Recent years should have a flatter profile with October "tail" extending.

4. When in the Calendar Year Do Fires Occur? (Density Plot)

What it shows: Each year as a separate curve showing how fire activity is distributed across the calendar (June=day 152, July=183, ..., October=305). If curves are shifting right and spreading, the season is starting later AND lasting longer. Wide curves = long season; narrow curves = short season.
💡 Look for shift patterns: Are 2024-2025 curves different from 2018-2019? Does the tail extend further right?

🎯 What This Creative Approach Reveals

Instead of just showing "evolution over time," these visualizations directly measure:

✓ Season length in days - can you see an upward trend?
✓ Season boundary shifts - are start dates moving earlier, end dates later?
✓ Shape changes - is the seasonal profile flattening or extending?
✓ Calendar distribution - where is fire activity concentrated, and is it spreading?

📊 Data Transformation & Methodology

Season Definition: Fire season begins when monthly detections exceed 500 (the threshold where fire activity becomes substantial), and ends when it drops below 500. This allows each year to have its own natural start/end dates rather than assuming fixed June-October.

Calendar Days: June 1 = day 152, July 1 = day 182, etc. This allows cross-year comparison on a common "year" scale.

Normalization: In the intensity profile, each year's detections are normalized to [0,1] so that high-fire years (2018) and low-fire years (2022) can be compared on the same scale—revealing SHAPE changes independent of magnitude.

Density Estimation: Kernel density estimation (bandwidth=10 days) smooths the distribution to reveal underlying patterns.
