🔥 Wildfire Season Expansion: Interactive Analysis

Are fire seasons starting earlier AND lasting longer? Explore the evidence with interactive D3 visualizations.

1. Season Length Trajectory (Interactive Trend)

Interaction: Hover over points to see exact season dates. Drag to select a year range and highlight those seasons. This reveals if recent years consistently show longer seasons.
💡 Hover over points • Drag to brush years • Watch for upward trend = seasons getting longer

2. Season Boundaries: Start vs End Dates (Linked View)

Interaction: Hover over lines to highlight them. Slope direction shows season length directly. Steeper slopes (longer lines extending right) = longer seasons. Hover one view and the other updates.
💡 Hover to highlight • Red = recent years extending right longer
💡 Same data, different view • Notice October encroachment

3. Seasonal Intensity Profile (Normalized, Interactive)

Interaction: Click "Show Early Years" and "Show Recent Years" buttons to toggle between eras. Watch how the profile shape changes—flatter/wider = longer distributed season. October presence (right tail) clearly extends in recent years.
💡 Click buttons to compare eras • Watch October tail grow • More spread = longer season

4. October Growth Over Time (Direct Evidence)

Interaction: Hover over bars to see exact values. Notice the strong upward trend line. This is the smoking gun evidence: October fires have grown 105% from 2019 to 2024, clearly showing the "lasting longer" pattern.
💡 Hover for exact values • Red upward trend = October expanding • Clear evidence of "lasting longer"

🎯 What the Interactive Visualizations Reveal

By exploring these linked, interactive views, you can see:

✓ Season lengths are increasing: The scatter plot trend line slopes upward
✓ Seasons extend further into October: Slope chart right endpoints move right in recent years
✓ Seasonal profiles are flattening: Recent years show wider, less peaked distributions
✓ October is the primary driver: Bar chart shows 105% growth from 2019-2024

📊 Interaction Value

These visualizations cannot be effectively shown as static plots because:

• Linked highlighting: Hovering one view to highlight corresponding data in another reveals connections
• Brushing/filtering: Selecting year ranges dynamically updates to show patterns
• Toggle views: Comparing eras side-by-side through interactivity reveals shape changes
• Rich tooltips: Exact dates and values on hover provide precision without cluttering
