📊 Data-Driven Insights

  • Consistent 5-month season: Fire season remains June-October across all 8 years analyzed. No earlier May or later November fires detected above baseline.
  • October fires increasing: Late-season October fires grew 105% from 2019 (840 detections) to 2024 (1,728), supporting the "lasting longer" hypothesis.
  • June remains peak season: June averages 28,396 detections (largest monthly total), indicating strong spring initiation.
  • Seasonal volatility: Year-to-year variations suggest climate/weather factors (2018 was exceptionally active; 2019-2022 showed decline).
  • Late-season persistence: October consistently has 1,000+ fire detections, indicating fires now occur in traditionally off-season months.

📈 How to Define Fire Season

  • Duration metric: Count consecutive months with ≥100 fire detections (currently 5 months: Jun-Oct)
  • Intensity metric: Sum of fire power/detections across season to measure "active season strength"
  • Extension metric: Month-over-month growth in October as proxy for "lasting longer" trend
  • Shift metric: Compare peak fire month across years to detect earlier/later start timing
