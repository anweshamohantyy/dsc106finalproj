🔥 Are Wildfire Seasons Expanding?

Interactive D3 analysis testing if fire seasons are starting earlier and lasting longer.

1. Season Length Over Time (Hover to Explore)

Interactive: Hover over points to see exact season dates. The orange dashed trend line shows if seasons are growing longer.
💡 Hover over points • Upward trend = seasons getting longer

2. Season Boundaries: Start vs End (Linked Highlighting)

Interactive: Hover over lines to highlight them. Red = recent years extending further right (longer seasons). Blue = early years with shorter seasons.
💡 Hover over slopes • Steeper right = longer season • Notice red extending further right

3. October Growth: The Smoking Gun

Interactive: Hover over bars. October detections grew 105% from 2019→2024. This is the clearest evidence of "lasting longer."
💡 Hover for exact values • Red trend line slopes upward sharply

4. Seasonal Intensity Profiles (Click to Toggle Eras)

Interactive: Click buttons to compare how fire activity spreads across the season. Early years peak sharply; recent years show flatter, longer profiles with visible October tails.
💡 Click buttons to toggle • Watch profile shape change • Flatter = longer season
✓ Key Finding: The visualizations clearly demonstrate that fire seasons are indeed lasting longer. October fires show a 105% increase from 2019 to 2024, and the seasonal intensity profiles show recent years with flatter, more extended distributions. The slope chart shows recent years' endpoints extending further into October than early years.
