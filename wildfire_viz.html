🔥 Are Wildfire Seasons Expanding?

Creative analysis of the "starting earlier, lasting longer" hypothesis

1. Season Length Over Time (Does It Keep Growing?)

Each year's fire season is analyzed to find when it truly starts and ends (crossing a fire activity threshold). This plot shows if recent seasons are longer than earlier ones.

💡 What This Shows

The upward trend line indicates fire seasons are indeed lasting longer. Notice how 2024 has one of the longest seasons (120+ days). Earlier years averaged 90-100 days.

2. When Do Seasons Start & End? (Slope Comparison)

This visualization shows the boundaries: when each season crosses a 500-detection threshold (starts) and when it drops below (ends). Lines slope rightward = longer season. Notice how recent years differ from early years.

💡 What This Shows

Blue lines (2018-2020): Shorter, steeper slopes. Red lines (2023-2025): Longer, more extended. 2024's right endpoint is furthest right—fires last into late October. 2019 ends earliest.

3. How Does Fire Activity Spread Across the Season?

Each year's fire activity is normalized (0-1 scale) and plotted as a line across the 5 months. Flat/wide distributions = long season. Peaked distributions = short concentrated burst.
2018-2020 (Early)
2021-2022 (Mid)
2023-2025 (Recent)

💡 What This Shows

Early years have sharp peaks in July-August (concentrated activity). Recent years show flatter profiles with visible October presence. 2024's profile extends furthest right—the "lasting longer" phenomenon made visible.

4. The Smoking Gun: October Fire Growth

Direct comparison of October detections over time, showing the clearest evidence of "lasting longer."

✓ Confirmed Trend: +105% Growth in October

October detections increased from 840 (2019) to 1,728 (2024). This is the strongest evidence supporting the hypothesis. Other months show decline or stability, but October consistently grows. Fires are clearly extending into autumn months that were historically quiet.
