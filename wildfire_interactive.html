🔥 Wildfire Seasons: Are They Starting Earlier and Lasting Longer?

Interactive analysis of North American fire detection data (2018-2025)

Visualization Goal: Explore temporal and spatial patterns in wildfire seasons to test the hypothesis that fire seasons are expanding. Use the interactive elements below to discover how season characteristics have evolved and where shifts are most pronounced.

Key Findings

✓ Lasting Longer (Confirmed)

October fire detections increased 105% from 2019 (840) to 2024 (1,728), showing clear evidence that fire seasons extend further into autumn. This is the most compelling evidence of the "lasting longer" trend.

? Starting Earlier (Inconclusive)

June remains the peak month but shows high variability (1,600-7,023 fires/year). No significant earlier May activity in this dataset. The "starting earlier" claim would require data from April/May to verify.

📍 Geographic Persistence

Fires concentrated in western regions (100°W-120°W, 35°N-55°N) across all months. October fires shift slightly northward compared to June-July, suggesting different fire regimes or travel distances.

📈 Season Definition

Fire season defined as consecutive months with ≥100 detections. Current: 5 months (June-October). To claim "longer," would need historical data (pre-2018) showing seasons shorter than 5 months.
